Chapter 3 · The clever bit

We split the job in half

The robot does the part Discord allows. You do the part Discord can't tell apart from a normal user.

Robot's job

Set the stage

  • ✓ Open empty DM channels with every lead
  • ✓ Watch your account in real-time
  • ✓ Send the templated follow-ups
  • ✓ Space messages with cooldowns

All of this is allowed — Discord doesn't block it.

Your job

Break the ice

  • ✓ Open Discord (your normal app)
  • ✓ Click each new empty DM in your sidebar
  • ✓ Hit the native Wave to X button
  • ✓ Move on. We do the rest.

Discord sees a human clicking — exactly what they want.

Chapter 4 · A campaign, end to end

From lead list to first reply

1

Build your campaign

Pick which Discord accounts will send. Choose servers to scrape members from. Write your message template. Set how many sends per hour and per day.

2

Click "Prepare DM channels"

We silently create empty DMs with every cold lead on Discord's side. No message yet — just an empty conversation. Discord allows this freely (no captcha).

⚡ Takes seconds, works for any number of leads
3

Open your real Discord

Switch to the account we just used. All the new empty DMs are sitting in your sidebar. Click each one and hit the Wave to X button. That's the green light Discord shows for empty conversations.

4

We detect each wave instantly

As you wave, your rows turn green in our board — usually within 2-3 seconds. We're listening to Discord live, the same way their own app does.

5

Hit Start campaign

The button enables once everything's green. The engine sends your real templated message through every warm DM, with cooldown spacing between sends. Discord doesn't captcha-wall messages in channels that already have history — so it flows cleanly.

6

Replies land in the Unibox

Every response — from every account, every conversation — shows up in one inbox. Reply, archive, mark read. Done.

Chapter 5 · The graveyard

What we tried first (and why it failed)

We spent a lot of versions chasing fully-automated cold DMs. Every clever path got beaten.

🧩

Captcha-solving services (2Captcha, CapSolver, AntiCaptcha)

We paid services to solve captchas for us. They solved on a worker's phone, sent us the token, and we played it back. Discord rejected the playback because its captchas are bound to the specific device that originated the request.

Result: ❌ doesn't transfer between devices

🎭

Faking a real browser's fingerprint

We made our backend's network calls look identical to Chrome's, down to the encryption handshake. Discord's anti-fraud uses signals deeper than the network layer, so it still flagged us.

Result: ❌ Discord sees through it

🤖

A robot driving a real browser

We ran an actual Chrome browser on the server, logged in as each account, and tried to drive Discord's web UI like a robot. But Discord's site simply refuses to render an empty DM page for a stranger — so the bot couldn't find anything to click.

Result: ❌ Discord's UI gates the cold-DM page

👁️

Solving captchas from inside our app

We even built a live remote-control window so you could solve captchas in our web app. Cool tech — but we learned that on an already-flagged account, solving the captcha is the signal Discord uses to revoke the session entirely.

Result: ❌ solving accelerates the ban
